Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Concepts

This page provides a simple and clear understanding of Carbon Emissions, GHG Protocols, and Scope 1, 2, and 3 categories used in corporate emission tracking.

What is Carbon Emission?

Carbon emissions refer to the release of greenhouse gases (GHGs) such as CO₂, CH₄, N₂O into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat and contribute to climate change. Industries, transportation, fuel consumption, electricity use, and waste management all generate emissions.

What is the GHG Protocol?

The GHG Protocol is the world's most widely used standard for measuring, managing, and reporting greenhouse gas emissions. It divides emissions into three main categories:

  • Scope 1 – Direct Emissions
  • Scope 2 – Indirect Emissions (Purchased Energy)
  • Scope 3 – Other Indirect Emissions
Scope 1 – Direct Emissions

Emissions released from sources that are directly owned or controlled by the company.

Examples:
  • Diesel consumed by company-owned vehicles
  • Fuel used in DG sets and heavy machinery (HEMV, drills, pumps)
  • LPG used in the canteen or inside company premises
  • Fugitive emissions from AC refrigerant leakage (R32/R410 gases)
Scope 2 – Indirect Emissions from Purchased Energy

Emissions from electricity, steam, heat, or cooling purchased from outside sources.

Examples:
  • Electricity consumed in offices, processing plants, lighting, workshops
  • Energy used in water treatment plants or mining operations
Scope 3 – Other Indirect Emissions

Emissions that occur from activities not directly controlled by the company but related to operations.

Examples:
  • Employee commuting (two-wheelers, buses, cars)
  • Business travel (flights, trains, taxis)
  • Transportation of raw materials and finished products
  • Waste disposal (tyres, lubricants, batteries)
  • Purchased goods (steel, tyres, PVC boxes, lubricants)
